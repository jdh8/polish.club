# Design notes

Working notes on bidding-design decisions, kept outside the published book.

## Useful space principle

Meanings are assigned to calls by the [useful space principle][usp]: the
cheapest steps go to the hands that most need room to explore, while
well-described or sign-off hands spend the dearer steps.  Never burn a cheap
step on a hand that will not use the room.

This is almost an axiom when **responding to an artificial call**, where the
steps carry no natural meaning, so the allocation is entirely free.  (For
natural suit-showing the suit already fixes the step, leaving little freedom.)

**Tension — right-siding.**  Right-siding can override the principle.  In a
stopper-ask, letting opener declare notrump (so the opening lead runs up to the
stopper) wants opener to bid notrump *with* the stopper, even though that spends
a cheap step on a hand that needs little further room.

[usp]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Useful_space_principle
