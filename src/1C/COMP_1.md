# Competing at the 1-level

The design of the responses already bears competitive bidding in mind.  We
somehow ignore the meaning of overcalls to deal with artificial and psychic
bids.  We reorder major suit bids with Transfer Walsh to get a "support double"
by accepting the transfer.

## Over a double

We treat 1ظآث as a weak notrump more than a club opening.  Club support is not
very effective.  We have a transfer scheme for all levels below 3NT.

- **1ظآخظآح:** Transfer to the next strain, a major suit.
- **1ظآب:** Transfer to diamonds for the assumed misfit.
- **1NT:** Transfer to clubs for the assumed fit.
- **2NT through 3ظآح:** A weak or strong transfer of 6+ cards.
- **3ظآب:** Transfer Gambling 3NT.

Notrump is cuebid as its natural meaning is not very useful after a double.
Transfer structures start there ├ب la Rubens.

| 1ظآث(X) | - |
|-------|---|
| XX    | NF BAL G/T, 10+
| 1ظآخ!   | TRF, 7+, 4+ظآح
| 1ظآح!   | TRF, 7+, 4+ظآب
| 1ظآب!   | TRF, 7+, 5+ظآخ
| 1NT!  | TRF, 7+, 5+ظآث
| 2ظآث    | PRE, 0--7, 5+ظآث
| 2X    | PRE, 0--7, 6+#
| 2NT!  | TRF, PRE 7+ظآث or FG 6+ظآث
| 3ظآث!   | TRF, PRE 7+ظآخ or FG 6+ظآخ
| 3ظآخ!   | TRF, PRE 7+ظآح or FG 6+ظآح
| 3ظآح!   | TRF, PRE 7+ظآب or FG 6+ظآب
| 3ظآب!   | Gambling, SOL 7+ suit with no stopper outside
| 3NT!  | Gambling, SOL 7+ suit with one stopper outside

## Over a suit

Bidding clubs here is more important than (X) because we often need to compete
to 3ظآث.

| 1ظآث(1Y) | - |
|--------|---|
| X!     | TRF, 7+, 4+ next suit
| 1ظآح!    | TRF, 7+, 4+ظآب
| 1ظآب!    | TRF to 1NT, 8+, 0--3ظآب, 0--3ظآح
| 1NT    | NAT, 8--10
| 2X     | NF, 8--11, 5+#
| 2YZ!   | TRF, 6+ next suit
| 2ظآب!    | INV+ T/O, no other suitable call
| 2NT    | NAT INV, 10--11
| 3X     | FG, 6+#
| 3YZ    | INV+ TRF, 6+ next suit
| 3ظآب!    | Gambling, SOL 7+ suit w/o stopper

The whole bidding structure leans toward hearts over (1ظآب).  A well-known example
is the negative double.

| 1ظآث(1ظآب) | - |
|--------|---|
| X!     | TRF, 7+, 4+ظآح
| 2ظآح     | NF, usually 6+ظآح
| 3ظآح     | INV, 6+ظآح
| 3ظآب!    | FG, 6+ظآح

## Transfer Walsh

Transfer to major suits has the same meaning as major suit responses to 1ظآث.
Opener usually bids as if responder had bid the suit naturally.  However, opener
has a new option: accept the transfer at 1M.  This option *steals* the support
double when advancer passes.

Here I only list support rebids for brevity.  The complete set of responses is
at [1ظآث-1M](../1C/1M.md).

Transfer Walsh in competition is also called Cachalot in French.

| 1ظآث(1ظآخ)X- | 7+, 4+ظآح |
|----------|---------|
| 1ظآح       | SUPP, 12--17, 3=ظآح
| 2ظآخ!      | FG, 3+ظآح
| 2ظآح       | MIN, 12--14, 4=ظآح
| 3ظآح       | INV, 15--17, 4+ظآح

| 1ظآث(1ظآخ)1ظآح- or<br>1ظآث(1ظآح)X- | 7+, 4+ظآب |
|:-------------------------|---------|
| 1ظآب                       | SUPP, 12--17, 3=ظآب
| 2ظآخ!                      | FG, 3+ظآب
| 2ظآب                       | MIN, 12--14, 4=ظآب
| 3ظآب                       | INV, 15--17, 4+ظآب

The cuebid is a useful tool to ask for stopper by ceding notrump declaration. I
decide to have a dual meaning 2ظآح, similar to 4SF.  It asks for a stopper by
default.  Rebidding the suit shows a natural suit.

| 1ظآث(1ظآح)X- | 7+, 4+ظآب |
|----------|---------|
| 2ظآح!      | INV+, ask for stopper; FG, 5+ظآح
| 3ظآح!      | FG, 5+ظآخ, 5+ظآث

## Responder passes

Responder is strictly limited, and 1ظآث is prone to psychic bids.  Therefore,
there are some quirks about cuebids:

- Responder's cuebid (not to a X) is natural.
- Opener's cuebid after X is natural.
- Opener's 2NT is unusual because X includes 21+ "penalty".

| 1ظآث(1Y)P- | - |
|----------|---|

| 1Z       | 15--20, 4+#
| 1NT      | 18--20, usually likely stop
| 2ظآث       | 12--20, 5+ظآث
| 2X       | 20--23, usually 5+#
